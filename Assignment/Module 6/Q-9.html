Q. 9 - How is Cell Padding different from Cell Spacing?

  • Cell padding and cell spacing are two attributes used to control the spacing and layout of tables in HTML.
  • Cell padding is used to specify the space between the content of a table cell and the cell's border. It can be applied to individual cells or to the entire table. For example, the following HTML code sets a padding of 10 pixels for all cells in the table:

    • Cell 1 Cell 2
    Cell 3 Cell 4

  • Cell spacing, on the other hand, is used to specify the space between adjacent cells in a table. It applies to the space between all cells in the table, not just individual cells. For example, the following HTML code sets a spacing of 5 pixels between all cells in the table:

    • Cell 1 Cell 2
    Cell 3 Cell 4

  • To summarize, cell padding controls the space between the content of a cell and its border, while cell spacing controls the space between adjacent cells in a table.
