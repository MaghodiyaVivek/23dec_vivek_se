Q. 17 - What are the different tags to separate sections of text?

  • There are several tags that can be used to separate sections of text, depending on the context and purpose of the text. Here are some commonly used tags:

    1. Heading tags: These are used to denote the heading or title of a section. They are denoted by the < h1>, < h2>, < h3>, < h4>, < h5>, and < h6> tags, with < h1> being the largest and most important heading.
    2. Paragraph tags: These are used to denote a block of text or a paragraph. They are denoted by the < p> tag.
    3. List tags: These are used to denote lists of items. There are two types of list tags: ordered and unordered. Ordered lists are denoted by the < ol> tag and unordered lists are denoted by the < ul> tag. Each item in a list is denoted by the < li> tag.
    4. Division tags: These are used to divide a page into sections or areas. They are denoted by the < div> tag.
    5. nchor tags: These are used to create hyperlinks to other pages or sections within a page. They are denoted by the < a> tag.

  • These tags are commonly used in HTML to structure and format web pages.
